Problem 2.61.

Suppose our universe of discourse is the set of integers.
  1. Provide an example of a predicate \(P(x)\) such that “For all \(x\text{,}\) \(P(x)\)” is true.
  2. Provide an example of a predicate \(Q(x)\) such that “For all \(x\text{,}\) \(Q(x)\)” is false while “There exists \(x\) such that \(Q(x)\)” is true.
