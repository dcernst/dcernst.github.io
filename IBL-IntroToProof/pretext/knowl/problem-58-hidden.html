Let \(S\) and \(T\) be sets. Determine whether each of the following statements is true or false. If the statement is true, prove it. If the statement is false, provide a counterexample.
  1. \(\displaystyle \mathcal{P}(S\cap T) \subseteq \mathcal{P}(S)\cap\mathcal{P}(T)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \mathcal{P}(S)\cap\mathcal{P}(T)\subseteq \mathcal{P}(S\cap T)\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \mathcal{P}(S\cup T)\subseteq \mathcal{P}(S)\cup\mathcal{P}(T)\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \mathcal{P}(S)\cup\mathcal{P}(T)\subseteq \mathcal{P}(S\cup T)\)
