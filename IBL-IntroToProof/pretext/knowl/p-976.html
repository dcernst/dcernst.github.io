Theorem 6.6 states that we can write every natural number greater than 1 as a product of primes, but it does not say that the primes and the number of times each prime appears are unique. To prove uniqueness, we will need Euclid’s Lemma (Theorem 6.15). To prove Euclid’s Lemma, we will utilize a special case of Bézout’s Lemma (Theorem 6.13), the proof of which relies on the following result, known as the Division Algorithm.
