Theorem 4.24. Principle of Complete Mathematical Induction.

Let \(P(1), P(2), P(3), \ldots\) be a sequence of statements, one for each natural number. Assume that
  1. \(P(1)\) is true, and
  2. For all \(k \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) if \(P(j)\) is true for all \(j\in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(j \leq k\text{,}\) then \(P(k+1)\) is true.
Then \(P(n)\) is true for all \(n\in\mathbb{N}\text{.}\)
