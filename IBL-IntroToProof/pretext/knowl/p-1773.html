The symbol “\(=\)” means “equals”. Do not write \(A=B\) unless you mean that \(A\) actually equals \(B\text{.}\) This guideline seems obvious, but there is a great temptation to be sloppy. In calculus, for example, some people might write \(f(x)=x^{2}=2x\) (which is false), when they really mean that “if \(f(x)=x^{2}\text{,}\) then \(f'(x)=2x\text{.}\)”
