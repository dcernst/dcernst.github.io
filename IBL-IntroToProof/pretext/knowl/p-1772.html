Know the difference between statements and objects. A mathematical object is a thing, a noun, such as a set, an element, a number, an ordered pair, a vector space, etc. Objects either exist or do not exist. Statements, on the other hand, are mathematical sentences: they are either true or false. When you see or write a cluster of math symbols, be sure you know whether it is an object (e.g., “\(x^2+3\)” ) or a statement (e.g., “\(x^2+3\lt 7\)”). One way to tell is that every mathematical statement includes a verb, such as \(=\text{,}\) \(\leq\text{,}\) \(\in\text{,}\) “divides”, etc.
