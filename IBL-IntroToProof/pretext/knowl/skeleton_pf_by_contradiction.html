Here is what the general structure for a proof by contradiction looks like if we are trying to prove the proposition \(P\text{.}\)

Proof.

[State any upfront assumptions.] For sake of a contradiction, assume \(\neg P\text{.}\) \(\ldots\) [Use definitions and known results to derive some \(Q\) and its negation \(\neg Q\text{.}\)]} \(\ldots\) This is a contradiction. Therefore, \(P\text{.}\)
