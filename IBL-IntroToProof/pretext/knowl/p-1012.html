Thus they were stunned when one of their own—Hippasus of Metapontum (c. 5th century BCE)—discovered that the side and the diagonal of a square are incommensurable. That is, the ratio of the length of the diagonal to the length of the side is irrational. Indeed, if the side of the square has length \(a\text{,}\) then the diagonal will have length \(a\sqrt{2}\text{;}\) the ratio is \(\sqrt{2}\) (see Figure 6.19).
