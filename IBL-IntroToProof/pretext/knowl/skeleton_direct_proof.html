Skeleton Proof 2.49. Proof of \(A\implies B\) by direct proof.

If you want to prove the implication \(A\implies B\) via a direct proof, then the structure of the proof is as follows.

Proof.

[State any upfront assumptions.] Assume \(A\text{.}\) \(\ldots\) [Use definitions and known results to derive \(B\)] \(\ldots\) Therefore, \(B\text{.}\)
