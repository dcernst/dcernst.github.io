Up until this point, you may have only encountered functions as an algebraic rule, e.g., \(f(x)=x^{2}-1\text{,}\) for transforming one real number into another. However, we can study functions in a much broader context. The basic building blocks of a function are a first set and a second set, say \(X\) and \(Y\text{,}\) and a “correspondence” that assigns every element of \(X\) to exactly one element of \(Y\text{.}\) Let’s take a look at the actual definition.
