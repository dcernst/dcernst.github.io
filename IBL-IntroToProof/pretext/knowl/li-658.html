Item 18.

Use a symbol to mean only one thing. Once you use the letter \(x\) once, its meaning is fixed for the duration of your proof. You cannot use \(x\) to mean anything else. There is an exception to this guideline. Sometimes a proof will include multiple subproofs that are distinct from each other. In this case, you can reuse a variable or symbol as long as it is clear to the reader that you have concluded with the previous subproof and have moved onto a new subproof.
