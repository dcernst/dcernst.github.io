Problem 2.64.

Suppose the universe of discourse is the set of real numbers and consider the predicate \(F(x,y)\coloneqq\) “\(x=y^2\)”. Interpret the meaning of each of the following statements.
  1. There exists \(x\) such that there exists \(y\) such that \(F(x,y)\text{.}\)
  2. There exists \(y\) such that there exists \(x\) such that \(F(x,y)\text{.}\)
  3. For all \(y\text{,}\) for all \(x\text{,}\) \(F(x,y)\text{.}\)
