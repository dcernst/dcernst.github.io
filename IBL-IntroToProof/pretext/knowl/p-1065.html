If \(R\) is clear from the context, we will usually write \(\tcboxmath{\rel(a)}\) in place of \(\rel(a,R)\text{.}\) In terms of digraphs, \(\rel(a)\) is the collection of vertices that have a directed edge pointing towards them from the vertex labeled by \(a\text{.}\) In graph theory, this collection of vertices is called the out neighborhood of \(a\) and each such vertex is called an out neighbor.
