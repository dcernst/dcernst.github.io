Item 21.

Make your counterexamples concrete and specific. Proofs need to be entirely general, but counterexamples should be concrete. When you provide an example or counterexample, make it as specific as possible. For a set, for example, you must specify its elements, and for a function you must specify the corresponding relation (possibly an algebraic rule) and its domain and codomain. Do not say things like “\(f\) could be one-to-one but not onto”; instead, provide an actual function \(f\) that is one-to-one but not onto.
