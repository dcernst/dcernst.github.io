Suppose the universe of discourse is the set of people and consider the predicate \(M(x,y)\coloneqq\) “\(x\text{ is married to } y\)”. We can interpret the formal statement “For all \(x\text{,}\) there exists \(y\) such that \(M(x,y)\)” as meaning “Everybody is married to somebody.” Interpret the meaning of each of the following statements in a similar way.
  1. For all \(x\text{,}\) there exists \(y\) such that \(M(x,y)\text{.}\)
  2. There exists \(y\) such that for all \(x\text{,}\) \(M(x,y)\text{.}\)
  3. For all \(x\text{,}\) for all \(y\text{,}\) \(M(x,y)\text{.}\)
  4. There exists \(x\) such that there exists \(y\) such that \(M(x,y)\text{.}\)
