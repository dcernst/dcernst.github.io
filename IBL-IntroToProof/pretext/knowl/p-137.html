The proposition “If \(A\text{,}\) then \(B\)” is true if both \(A\) and \(B\) are true, or \(A\) is false; expressed symbolically as \(\tcboxmath{A \implies B}\) and called a conditional proposition (or implication). In this case, \(A\) is called the hypothesis and \(B\) is called the conclusion. Note that \(A \implies B\) may also be read as “\(A\) implies \(B\)”, “\(A\) only if \(B\)”, “\(B\) if \(A\)”, or “\(B\) whenever \(A\)”.
