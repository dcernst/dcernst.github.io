Loosely speaking, what Theorem 9.54 says is that the open interval \((0,1)\) is “bigger” in terms of the number of elements it contains than the natural numbers and even the rational numbers. This shows that there are infinite sets of different sizes! We now know there is at least one uncountable set, namely the interval \((0,1)\text{.}\) The next three results are useful for finding other uncountable sets. For the first theorem, try a proof by contradiction and take a look at Theorem 9.42.
