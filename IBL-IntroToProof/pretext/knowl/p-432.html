Note that if we want to prove \(A=B\text{,}\) then we have to do two separate subproofs: one for \(A\subseteq B\) and one for \(B\subseteq A\text{.}\) Be sure to make it clear to the reader where these subproofs begin and end. One approach is to label each subproof with “\((\subseteq)\)” and “\((\supseteq)\)” (including the parentheses), respectively.
