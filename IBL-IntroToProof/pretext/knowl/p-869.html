To prove the next theorem, let \(a\lt b\text{,}\) utilize Theorem 5.55 on \(b-a\) to obtain \(N\in\mathbb{N}\) such that \(\frac{1}{N}\lt b-a\text{,}\) and then apply Theorem 5.56 to \(Na\) to conclude that there exists \(n\in\mathbb{N}\) such that \(n\leq Na\lt n+1\text{.}\) Lastly, argue that \(\frac{n+1}{N}\) is the rational number you seek.
