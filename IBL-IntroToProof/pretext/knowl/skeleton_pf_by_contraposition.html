Skeleton Proof 2.49. Proof of \(A\implies B\) by contraposition.

If you want to prove the implication \(A\implies B\) by proving its contrapositive \(\neg B\implies \neg A\) instead, then the structure of the proof is as follows.

Proof.

[State any upfront assumptions.] We will utilize a proof by contraposition. Assume \(\neg B\text{.}\) \(\ldots\) [Use definitions and known results to derive \(\neg A\)] \(\ldots\) Therefore, \(\neg A\text{.}\) We have proved the contrapositive, and hence if \(A\text{,}\) then \(B\text{.}\)
