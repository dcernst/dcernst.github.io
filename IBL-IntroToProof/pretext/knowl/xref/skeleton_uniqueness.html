Skeleton Proof 2.90. Direct Proof of \((\exists! x)P(x)\).

Here is the general structure for a direct proof of the proposition \((\exists! x)P(x)\text{.}\) Assume \(U\) is the universe of discourse.

Proof.

[State any upfront assumptions.] \(\ldots\) \(\ldots\) [Use definitions, axioms, and previous results to deduce that an \(x\) exists for which \(P(x)\) is true; or if you have an \(x\) that works, just verify that it does.] \(\ldots\) Therefore, there exists \(x\in U\) such that \(P(x)\text{.}\) Now, suppose \(x_1,x_2\in U\) such that \(P(x_1)\) and \(P(x_2)\text{.}\) \(\ldots\) [Prove that \(x_1=x_2\text{.}\)] \(\ldots\) This implies that there exists a unique \(x\) such that \(P(x)\text{.}\)
