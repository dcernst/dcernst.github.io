Note the difference between ordinary induction (Theorems 4.2 and Theorem 4.10) and complete induction. For the induction step of complete induction, we are not only assuming that \(P(k)\) is true, but rather that \(P(j)\) is true for all \(j\) from 1 to \(k\text{.}\) Despite the name, complete induction is not any stronger or more powerful than ordinary induction. It is worth pointing out that anytime ordinary induction is an appropriate proof technique, so is complete induction. So, when should we use complete induction?
