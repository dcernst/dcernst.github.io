Notice that if \(p\) is an accumulation point of \(A\text{,}\) then \(p\) may or may not be in \(A\text{.}\) Loosely speaking, \(p\) is an accumulation point of a set \(A\) if there are points in \(A\) arbitrarily close to \(p\text{.}\) That is, if we zoom in on \(p\text{,}\) we should always see points in \(A\) nearby.
