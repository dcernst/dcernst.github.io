Item 16.

Introduce every symbol you use. If you use the letter “\(k\text{,}\)” the reader should know exactly what \(k\) is. Good phrases for introducing symbols include “Let \(n\in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\)” “Let \(k\) be the least integer such that…,” “For every real number \(a\)…,” and “Suppose \(A\subseteq\mathbb{R}\ldots\)”.
