Using our abbreviations for the logical connectives and quantifiers, we can symbolically represent mathematical propositions. For example, the (true) proposition “There exists \(x \in \mathbb{R}\) such that \(x^2-1=0\)” becomes “\((\exists x \in \mathbb{R})(x^2-1=0)\text{,}\)” while the (false) proposition “For all \(x\in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) there exists \(y\in\mathbb{N}\) such that \(y\lt x\)” becomes “\((\forall x\in\mathbb{N})(\exists y\in\mathbb{N})(y\lt x)\text{.}\)”
