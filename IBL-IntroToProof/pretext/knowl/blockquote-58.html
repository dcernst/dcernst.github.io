A function \(f:A\to \mathbb{R}\) is continuous at a point \(c\in A\) if, for all \(\varepsilon>0\text{,}\) there exists \(\delta>0\) such that whenever \(|x-c|\lt \delta\) (and \(x\in A\)) it follows that \(|f(x)-f(c)|\lt \varepsilon\text{.}\) If \(f\) is continuous at every point in the domain \(A\text{,}\) then we say that \(f\) is continuous on \(A\text{.}\) 7
