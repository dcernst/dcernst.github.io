When we write \(x^2+y^2=1\text{,}\) we are implicitly defining a relation. In particular, the relation is the set of ordered pairs \((x,y)\) satisfying \(x^2+y^2=1\text{,}\) namely \(\{(x,y)\in \mathbb{R}^2 \mid x^2+y^2=1\}\text{.}\) The graph of this relation in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) is the unit circle centered at the origin in the plane as shown in FigureÂ 7.17.
