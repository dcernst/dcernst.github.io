Let \(X=\{\circ, \square,\triangle,\smiley\}\) and \(Y=\{a,b,c,d,e\}\text{.}\) For each of the following relations, draw the corresponding digraph and determine whether the relation represents a function from \(X\) to \(Y\text{,}\) \(Y\) to \(X\text{,}\) \(X\) to \(X\text{,}\) or does not represent a function. If the relation is a function, determine the domain, codomain, and range.
  1. \(\displaystyle f=\{(\circ, a),(\square,b),(\triangle,c),(\smiley,d)\}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle g=\{(\circ, a),(\square,b),(\triangle,c),(\smiley,c)\}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle h=\{(\circ, a),(\square,b),(\triangle,c),(\circ,d)\}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle k=\{(\circ, a),(\square,b),(\triangle,c),(\smiley,d),(\square,e)\}\)
  5. \(\displaystyle l=\{(\circ, e),(\square,e),(\triangle,e),(\smiley,e)\}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle m=\{(\circ, a),(\triangle,b),(\smiley,c)\}\)
  7. \(\displaystyle i=\{(\circ,\circ),(\square, \square),(\triangle,\triangle),(\smiley,\smiley)\}\)
  8. Define the relation \(\operatorname{happy}\) from \(Y\) to \(X\) via \((y,\smiley)\in\operatorname{happy}\) for all \(y\in Y\text{.}\)
