Use complete sentences. If you wrote a history essay in sentence fragments, the reader would not understand what you meant; likewise in mathematics you must use complete sentences, with verbs, to convey your logical train of thought. Some complete sentences can be written purely in mathematical symbols, such as equations (e.g., \(a^3=b^{-1}\)), inequalities (e.g., \(x\lt 5\)), and other relations (like \(5\big|10\) or \(7\in\mathbb{Z}\)). These statements usually express a relationship between two mathematical objects, like numbers or sets. However, it is considered bad style to begin a sentence with symbols. A common phrase to use to avoid starting a sentence with mathematical symbols is “We see that...”.
