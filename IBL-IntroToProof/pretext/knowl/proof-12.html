Proof

We proceed by induction.
  1. (i).
    Base step: [Verify that \(P(a)\) is true. This often, but not always, amounts to plugging \(n=a\) into two sides of some claimed equation and verifying that both sides are actually equal.]
  2. (ii).
    Inductive step: [Your goal is to prove “For all \(k\geq a\text{,}\) if \(P(k)\) is true, then \(P(k+1)\) is true.” ] Let \(k \geq a\) be an integer and assume that \(P(k)\) is true. [Do something to derive that \(P(k+1)\) is true.] Therefore, \(P(k+1)\) is true.
Thus, by induction, \(P(n)\) is true for all integers \(n \ge a\text{.}\)
