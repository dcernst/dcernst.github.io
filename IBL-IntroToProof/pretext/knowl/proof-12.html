Proof

We proceed by induction.
  1. Base step: [Verify that \(P(1)\) is true. This often, but not always, amounts to plugging \(n=1\) into two sides of some claimed equation and verifying that both sides are actually equal.]
  2. Inductive step: [Your goal is to prove “For all \(k\in\mathbb{N}\text{,}\) if \(P(k)\) is true, then \(P(k+1)\) is true.”] Let \(k\in\mathbb{N}\) and assume that \(P(k)\) is true. [Do something to derive that \(P(k+1)\) is true.] Therefore, \(P(k+1)\) is true.
Thus, by induction, \(P(n)\) is true for all \(n\in\mathbb{N}\text{.}\)
