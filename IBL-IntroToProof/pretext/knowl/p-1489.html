The upshot of Theorems 8.78 and Theorem 8.80 is that if \(f^{-1}\) is a function, then it is the only one satisfying the two-sided inverse property exhibited in Corollary 8.71 and Theorem 8.78. That is, inverse functions are unique when they exist. When the relation \(f^{-1}\) is a function, we call it the inverse function of \(f\text{.}\)
