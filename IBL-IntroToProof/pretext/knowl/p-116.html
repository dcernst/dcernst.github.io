In the previous section, we jumped in head first and attempted to prove several theorems in the context of number theory without a formal understanding of what it was we were doing. Likely, many issues bubbled to the surface. What is a proof? What sorts of statements require proof? What should a proof entail? How should a proof be structured? Let’s take a step back and do a more careful examination of what it is we are actually doing. In the the next two sections, we will introduce the basics of propositional logic—also referred to as propositional calculus or sometimes zeroth-order logic.
