We encountered \(\equiv_5\) in Problem 9.1.25 and discovered that there were five distinct sets of relatives. In particular, we have
\begin{align*} \rel(0) \amp = \{\ldots, -10, -5, 0, 5, 10,\ldots\}\\ \rel(1) \amp = \{\ldots, -9, -4, 1, 6, 11,\ldots\}\\ \rel(2) \amp = \{\ldots, -8, -3, 2, 7, 12,\ldots\}\\ \rel(3) \amp = \{\ldots, -7, -2, 3, 8, 13,\ldots\}\\ \rel(4) \amp = \{\ldots, -6, -1, 4, 9, 14,\ldots\}\text{.} \end{align*}
Notice that this collection forms a partition of \(\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) By Theorem 9.3.24, the relation \(\equiv_5\) must be an equivalence relation.
