In the inductive step, you need to reach \(P(k+1)\text{,}\) and you should ask yourself which of the previous cases you need to get there. If all you need is the statement \(P(k)\text{,}\) then ordinary induction is the way to go. If two preceding cases, \(P(k - 1)\) and \(P(k)\text{,}\) are necessary to reach \(P(k + 1)\text{,}\) then complete induction is appropriate. In the extreme, if one needs the full range of preceding cases (i.e., all statements \(P(1), P(2),\ldots,P(k)\)), then again complete induction should be utilized.
