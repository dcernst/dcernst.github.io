Negate each of the following propositions. Disregard the truth value and the universe of discourse.
  1. \(\displaystyle (\forall n\in\mathbb{N})(\exists m\in\mathbb{N})(m\lt n)\)
  2. For every \(y\in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) there exists \(x\in \mathbb{R}\) such that \(y=x^2\text{.}\)
  3. For all \(y\in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) if \(y\) is not negative, then there exists \(x\in\mathbb{R}\) such that \(y=x^2\text{.}\)
  4. For every \(x\in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) there exists \(y\in \mathbb{R}\) such that \(y=x^2\text{.}\)
  5. There exists \(x\in \mathbb{R}\) such that for all \(y\in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) \(y=x^2\text{.}\)
  6. There exists \(y\in \mathbb{R}\) such that for all \(x\in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) \(y=x^2\text{.}\)
  7. \(\displaystyle (\forall x,y,z\in\mathbb{Z})((xy \mbox{ is even} \wedge yz\mbox{ is even} )\implies xz\mbox{ is even} )\)
  8. There exists a married person \(x\) such that for all married people \(y\text{,}\) \(x\) is married to \(y\text{.}\)
